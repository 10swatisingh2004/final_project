Explore diseases, symptoms, precautions, medications, and more.

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

Description: Narrowing of coronary arteries due to plaque buildup, which can reduce blood flow to the heart.

General Symptoms: Chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue, dizziness.

General Precautions: Regular exercise, healthy diet, quit smoking, manage stress, monitor cholesterol levels.

Medications: Statins, aspirin, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, nitrates.

Learn More at CDC

Heart Failure

Description: A condition in which the heart can't pump blood effectively, leading to fluid buildup in the body.

General Symptoms: Shortness of breath, fatigue, swollen legs, rapid or irregular heartbeat.

General Precautions: Low-salt diet, regular monitoring of blood pressure, avoid alcohol, maintain a healthy weight.

Medications: ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, diuretics, aldosterone antagonists.

Learn More at CDC

Arrhythmia (e.g., Atrial Fibrillation)

Description: An abnormal heart rhythm that can cause irregular and often rapid heartbeats.

General Symptoms: Palpitations, shortness of breath, dizziness, fatigue.

General Precautions: Limit alcohol, monitor heart rate, reduce caffeine intake, manage stress.

Medications: Anticoagulants, beta-blockers, anti-arrhythmic medications.

Learn More at CDC

Stroke

Description: A stroke occurs when blood flow to part of the brain is blocked, leading to brain cell death.

General Symptoms: Sudden numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg (especially on one side), trouble speaking, confusion.

General Precautions: Control blood pressure, reduce cholesterol, avoid smoking, maintain a healthy weight.

Medications: Antiplatelet drugs, thrombolytics (for certain types of stroke).

Learn More at CDC

Epilepsy

Description: A neurological disorder that causes recurrent seizures due to abnormal brain activity.

General Symptoms: Uncontrolled shaking or jerking movements, confusion, loss of consciousness.

General Precautions: Avoid triggers like flashing lights or stress, follow treatment plans, wear safety gear if needed.

Medications: Anticonvulsants, sometimes surgery or a special diet may be prescribed.

Learn More at Epilepsy Foundation

Parkinson’s Disease

Description: A progressive nervous system disorder affecting movement due to a lack of dopamine in the brain.

General Symptoms: Tremors, stiffness, difficulty with balance, slow movement, facial expression changes.

General Precautions: Physical therapy, speech therapy, avoid falls, maintain a balanced diet.

Medications: Levodopa, dopamine agonists, MAO-B inhibitors, physical therapy.

Learn More at Parkinson's Foundation

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

Description: An inflammatory skin condition causing itching, redness, and sometimes blistering or crusting.

General Symptoms: Red, itchy patches, dryness, swelling, sometimes cracks or bleeding in the skin.

General Precautions: Use mild soap, moisturize regularly, avoid triggers, wear soft fabrics.

Medications: Topical corticosteroids, antihistamines, moisturizing creams.

Learn More at NIAMS

Psoriasis

Description: An autoimmune condition that causes the rapid build-up of skin cells, forming scaly patches.

General Symptoms: Red patches with thick, silvery scales, dry and cracked skin that may bleed, itching, burning sensations.

General Precautions: Avoid triggers, moisturize, reduce stress, avoid smoking and alcohol.

Medications: Topical treatments, biologics, phototherapy, systemic treatments.

Learn More at National Psoriasis Foundation

Acne

Description: A skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil, dead skin cells, and bacteria.

General Symptoms: Pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, cysts, and scarring, often on the face, back, and chest.

General Precautions: Regularly wash the skin, avoid touching the face, eat a balanced diet, reduce stress.

Medications: Topical retinoids, benzoyl peroxide, antibiotics, oral contraceptives for hormonal acne.

Learn More at American Academy of Dermatology

Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)

Description: A digestive disorder where stomach acid or bile irritates the food pipe lining.

General Symptoms: Heartburn, regurgitation, difficulty swallowing, chest pain.

General Precautions: Eat smaller meals, avoid lying down after eating, limit alcohol and caffeine, maintain a healthy weight.

Medications: Antacids, proton pump inhibitors, H2 blockers, prokinetics.

Learn More at Mayo Clinic

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

Description: A functional gastrointestinal disorder causing abdominal pain and altered bowel habits.

General Symptoms: Abdominal pain, bloating, diarrhea, constipation, mucus in stool.

General Precautions: Avoid high-fat foods, keep a food diary, reduce stress, exercise regularly.

Medications: Fiber supplements, antispasmodics, laxatives, anti-diarrheal medications, antidepressants.

Learn More at IBS Foundation

Peptic Ulcer Disease

Description: Sores that develop in the lining of the stomach, small intestine, or esophagus due to stomach acid.

General Symptoms: Burning stomach pain, bloating, nausea, heartburn, indigestion.

General Precautions: Avoid spicy foods, alcohol, and smoking, eat smaller meals, manage stress.

Medications: Proton pump inhibitors, H2 blockers, antibiotics (for H. pylori infection), antacids.

Learn More at Mayo Clinic

Asthma

Description: A chronic condition that causes inflammation and narrowing of the airways, leading to difficulty breathing.

General Symptoms: Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, coughing, especially at night or early morning.

General Precautions: Avoid triggers (dust, pollen, smoke), take prescribed inhalers, monitor air quality, stay active.

Medications: Inhaled corticosteroids, beta-agonists, leukotriene modifiers, oral steroids during flare-ups.

Learn More at CDC

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

Description: A progressive lung disease that makes it difficult to breathe, often caused by long-term exposure to irritants like smoking.

General Symptoms: Shortness of breath, chronic cough, mucus production, wheezing.

General Precautions: Quit smoking, use oxygen therapy, avoid respiratory infections, stay active.

Medications: Bronchodilators, corticosteroids, oxygen therapy, antibiotics (during exacerbations).

Learn More at CDC

Diabetes Mellitus (Type 2)

Description: A chronic condition where the body becomes resistant to insulin, leading to high blood sugar levels.

General Symptoms: Increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision, slow-healing wounds.

General Precautions: Maintain a healthy diet, exercise regularly, monitor blood sugar levels, avoid excessive sugar intake.

Medications: Metformin, sulfonylureas, insulin, GLP-1 receptor agonists.

Learn More at CDC

Hypothyroidism

Description: A condition where the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormones, leading to a slow metabolism.

General Symptoms: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin.

General Precautions: Take thyroid medication as prescribed, monitor hormone levels, maintain a balanced diet.

Medications: Levothyroxine (synthetic thyroid hormone).

Learn More at CDC

Osteoarthritis

Description: A degenerative joint disease where the cartilage in the joints wears down over time, leading to pain and stiffness.

General Symptoms: Joint pain, swelling, decreased range of motion, stiffness after periods of inactivity.

General Precautions: Maintain a healthy weight, stay active, use joint protection techniques, avoid excessive strain.

Medications: NSAIDs, acetaminophen, corticosteroid injections, physical therapy.

Learn More at CDC

Rheumatoid Arthritis

Description: An autoimmune disorder that causes inflammation in the joints, often leading to deformities and pain.

General Symptoms: Joint pain, swelling, stiffness (especially in the morning), fatigue, and loss of appetite.

General Precautions: Early treatment with disease-modifying drugs (DMARDs), regular physical activity, joint protection strategies.

Medications: Methotrexate, NSAIDs, corticosteroids, biologics, DMARDs.

Learn More at American College of Rheumatology

Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

Description: A bacterial infection that affects any part of the urinary system, including the kidneys, bladder, and urethra.

General Symptoms: Painful urination, frequent urination, cloudy urine, lower abdominal pain.

General Precautions: Drink plenty of water, urinate after intercourse, wipe from front to back, avoid irritants like scented hygiene products.

Medications: Antibiotics such as nitrofurantoin, trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole.

Learn More at CDC

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

Description: A long-term condition where the kidneys gradually lose function over time, leading to kidney failure.

General Symptoms: Fatigue, swelling in the legs and ankles, reduced urine output, difficulty concentrating, nausea.

General Precautions: Control blood pressure, limit salt intake, manage diabetes, stay hydrated, avoid nephrotoxic drugs.

Medications: ACE inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers (ARBs), diuretics, erythropoiesis-stimulating agents.

Learn More at CDC

  • Eat a balanced and nutritious diet
  • Stay hydrated and sleep well
  • Avoid self-medication
